Study Completed!

Congratulations, you made good progress in learning to read and write in a completely new, made-up language.

Thank you for your time. Your responses will help us to understand how people learn to become literate in a new language.

Debriefing

The purpose of this study was to explore how we learn to read and write a language when we are exposed to different dialects. Depending on the condition you were in, you might have been given words with just one pronunciation or words with different pronunciations.

While previous research has shown a link between exposure to dialects and poor literacy outcomes, our project aims to check whether this is still the case when we control for other factors such as educational and social background, which may give children advantages in exposure to language. We created the made-up language so everybody starts from the same point.

We thank you again for taking part in our study. If you have any further questions about this study, please feel free to contact Glenn Williams, at language.enquiries@abertay.ac.uk.
